Denver

City Overview

Denver is the capital city of Colorado. As Colorado's only true main urban hub, Denver is unique in its size and access to city lifestyle. With a population of around 715,522 people (United States Census Bureau, Denver County, Colorado 2024), Denver is far and away, the most populated area of Colorado. The city still provides a good indicator of its history as a western mining town that settled during the Pike's Peak gold rush in 1858 (Denver Visitors Bureau, Denver, Colorado history 2024). The governing bodies of Colorado work hard to preserve the history of the city. Even though it is a large urban area, people living in Denver enjoy its "small city" feel and its access to surrounding outdoor areas, especially the Rocky Mountains. For those who are staying in the city, there is really great access to different cultural activities and dining. Denver is also home to many sports teams and arenas, and Denverites are typically diehard fans of local teams.

Source: United States Census Bureau

Region and Economy

Denver is located directly east of the foothills of the Rocky Mountains, which make up the front range area of the state. Most of the biggest cities and populated areas lie in this state area. Being in this area makes Denver a great place for those who like to have fairly good access to the mountains or move along the front range to other popular cities in the state. The average income in Denver is $88,213 (United States Census Bureau, Denver County, Colorado 2024), which is only slightly less than the state average of $89,302 (Bureau, State of Colorado 2022) and is indicative of the city lifestyle enjoyed here.

Source: Visit Denver - History

Source: United States Census Bureau

Tourism and Activities

Denver itself is home to a lot of great dining and tourism. There are plenty of ways to spend time in the mile-high city, from its historical landmarks to museums and theatres. Many activities are available around the city, including the Museum of Natural History, the Denver Zoo, and one of the many art museums found in Denver. Denver also has several art districts where visitors can see local artists or enjoy many commissioned street art pieces. If dining is important, Denver is also home to some incredible restaurants. The city has many dining options, from casual eating to high-class restaurants. Denver recently had the honor of being added to the Michelin guide as one of the cities where restaurants are eligible to receive michelin stars, a highly regarded award for world-class restaurants (United States Census Bureau, Denver County, Colorado 2024).

Source: Visit Denver - Things to Do
